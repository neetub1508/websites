# High-Earner Hunt — Looking for the rare 8–10 (deep research, 2026-06-04)

> **Goal:** find calculator/tool niches scoring **8–10** = high volume + high CPC + LOW competition + a NEW site can actually rank.
> **Method:** 8 discovery agents mined 47 candidate niches across high-CPC domains (insurance, legal, loans, real estate, healthcare, B2B, emerging 2026 tax-law, wealth/crypto); top 14 sent to adversarial verification (6 completed; 8 stalled — flaky run, but the pattern was unanimous).

---

## 🎯 The verdict: **No candidate hit 8–10. None even reached 5. Realistic ceiling = 4/10.**

The top *preliminary* scores across all 47 were only 5–6, and every one of those was **downgraded to 3–4 on verification.** This isn't a sampling fluke — it's structural.

### Why 8–10 essentially doesn't exist for a new solo AdSense calculator site
Three legs must all be true at once. They're mutually exclusive in practice:

1. **The high CPC is an illusion for *calculators*.** The famous CPCs (Medicare $40–70, structured settlement $230–1,000, legal $150–500, "merchant account" $10–30) are bid on **transactional/lead-gen intent** (enrollment, "sell my payments," "lawyer near me"). A *calculator* page captures **informational** intent that monetizes at a **fraction** — realized display CPC is **$2–25**, not the headlines.
2. **The SERP leg is the killer.** Every high-CPC niche is **YMYL** (health/finance/legal/tax) — exactly where Google pins page 1 to **.gov + high-DR brands + verifiable expertise.** A new anonymous static site has the **worst** footing. And high-CPC niches are high-CPC *because* deep-pocketed advertisers compete → their SERPs are **fortified**.
3. **First-mover angles are mostly already closed** — funded operators (Ownwell $74M, calculator factories, established tool directories) are already there.

**Bottom line: chasing famous-high-CPC keywords is a trap.** The CPC you read about is bid on intent your calculator won't capture, on a SERP you can't crack.

## Best of a modest field (the two 4s)

### 🥇 No-Tax-on-Tips Occupation Eligibility Checker — fast, time-boxed sprint
- **The one genuinely unbuilt artifact** in the whole batch: a job-title → IRS TTOC *interactive checker* doesn't exist standalone yet, even though IRS publishes the 70+ occupation list.
- **Demand ramping now** toward a **Jan–Apr 2027** filing-season peak → a real, time-bound first-mover window.
- **Be first:** ship the 70+ per-occupation pages ("is a [bartender/nail tech/Uber driver] eligible?") + OBBBA §224 rules (≤$25k, MAGI phase-out, 2025–2028) **this summer**, before TurboTax/SmartAsset bolt on a checker.
- **Honest cap:** still a 4. YMYL, IRS.gov's free list on the SERP, ~$2–6 CPC, and it **sunsets in 2028** → a 2–3 year asset, not an evergreen compounder.

### 🥈 Net Settlement After Attorney Fees & Liens — durable, patient
- **Most durable:** evergreen (no seasonality/sunset), premium legal display RPM (~$5–25 effective — highest realized in the set).
- **Wedge:** lien-deduction *order* (fees before/after liens) + Medicare/Medicaid procurement-cost formula are genuinely underbuilt.
- **Honest cap:** core Legal-YMYL = hardest trust category for an anonymous domain; 12+ months patience; wedge is copyable.
